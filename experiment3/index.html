Experiment 3 - Images, Video, & Sound Art: Put Yourself Together

Description

Put Yourself Together

For this experiment, my goal was to create a moving puzzle from live video feed.

Controls
Mouse: click and drag puzzle pieces to move them around

Technical

There are two main classes: Puzzle and Piece. The Puzzle stores an array of pieces and an array of grid positions that the pieces start out in. Each piece is drawn by copying a portion of the input capture. Pieces store variables such as a boolean describing whether it is snapped somewhere on the grid, a goal position to lerp to, and a "wrongness" value (calculated by getting the distance between its original location and its current location), among others. The Puzzle class includes a function to sum up all of these wrongness values, which is then used to modify the sound of the scene and the frequency at which pieces break off. Whenever a piece is set to break off, there is a chance that the entire puzzle shatters, with every piece snapped to the grid breaking off and flying to a random location. This chance increases the higher the total wrongness value is.

Reflection

In the beginning, I had so much trouble thinking of an idea for this project. Even when I thought of building a video puzzle, I wasn't too inspired. But as I experimented more, I kept getting new ideas and the project really started to take form. One of the highs of the process was the moment when I accidentally made the puzzle very difficult to put back together. It was a happy accident that shaped all of my decisions after the fact. Although I had a lot of issues during the process, most of them I was able to fix quickly after some thinking or research online. It took a lot of time and work, but I think it was well worth it.

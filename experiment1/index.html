Experiment 1 - Vector Art, Animation & Interactivity

Description

My aim with this experiment was to create interesting visual effects accompanied by audio, with can be modified with simple mouse controls. My goal was to create visual and audio effects that worked with and enhanced one another.

Controls:
Left mouse button: hold and drag to modify the circles and their respective sound
Right mouse button: hold and drag to modify the squares and their respective sound
R: randomly generate a new seed
S: save canvas as a png

Technical

I render each shape one after the other on a 20x20 grid in the draw() function. When drawing a shape, color, opacity, size, and position are calculated using stored X and Y coordinates and some small amounts of randomization. The squares are a little more complicated than the circles, in that each vertex is randomly shifted in some direction according to these stored X and Y values. I detect whenever the mouse is dragged and check whether the left or right button is used, and update the appropriate X and Y values (i.e. I change the circles' stored X and Y values, if the left button is used). Additionally, I update the corresponding sound's volume and play speed according to the same X and Y values.

Reflection

I worked by myself on this project. At first, I didn't have any ideas of what I wanted to do aside from including some sort of modifiable audio. I wanted the visuals, whatever they were, to evoke a similar feeling to the accompanying audio. I think I was able to accomplish this, though not without some pain. One of the lowest points for me was getting to the start of the Innovate step and realizing that I didn't like anything about the visuals. Then, in making it look better, I worried it was too reminiscent of my inspirations. I still worry that it looks too similar, but I am happy with what I was able to create.
